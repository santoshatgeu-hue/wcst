After 10 correct sorts in a row, the rule changes silently. Notice it stopped working — and switch.
About the test. Developed by Esta Berg & David Grant (1948), the WCST measures cognitive flexibility and set-shifting, historically tied to prefrontal function. The clinically meaningful score is perseverative errors — continuing to sort by a rule that has stopped working.
This is a functional educational recreation with simplified scoring — not the Heaton clinical algorithm, and not a diagnostic instrument. Examiner mode reproduces the manual administration workflow (examiner sets the rule and delivers feedback); it does not add clinical norms.
